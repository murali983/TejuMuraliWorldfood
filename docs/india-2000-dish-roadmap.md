# India 2000 Dish Roadmap

## Goal
Build Tejaswi Murali World Foods into a credible Indian food platform with state-wise structure first, then expand toward more than 2,000 dishes without publishing thin or repetitive pages.

## Phase 1: Live now
- 36 public India hubs covering every state and union territory
- 180 seeded signature dishes across those hubs
- Homepage, XML sitemap, and HTML sitemap now point users and search engines into the India section
- Telangana already links to a detailed live recipe page for Hyderabadi Veg Dum Biryani

## Phase 2: Safe scale path
- Expand each state or union territory toward a target of 56 dishes
- Group recipe production into sub-clusters such as breakfast, festival sweets, curries, rice dishes, breads, chutneys, drinks, and street food
- Publish only reviewed long-form pages with culture notes, timings, nutrition, FAQ sections, storage advice, and internal links

## Why this structure is better than instantly publishing 2,000 pages
- It avoids low-value archive bloat
- It gives Google clearer topical clusters
- It keeps AdSense quality stronger
- It lets automation generate drafts while human review controls what goes live

1. Andhra Pradesh

## Editorial rule
Do not auto-publish every generated draft. Keep the India atlas public, but promote only reviewed recipes into the main recipe archive.
